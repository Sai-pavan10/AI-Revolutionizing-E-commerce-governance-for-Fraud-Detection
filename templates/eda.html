Visualizing patterns and relationships in e-commerce transaction data

Data Insights

This section provides visual insights into the e-commerce transaction data, highlighting patterns and relationships that may indicate fraudulent behavior. Explore these visualizations to better understand the key factors that differentiate legitimate and fraudulent transactions.

Tip: Look for significant differences between fraudulent and legitimate transactions across different variables, as these may serve as important predictors in your fraud detection models.
Fraud Distribution

Distribution of fraudulent vs. legitimate transactions in the dataset. Note the significant class imbalance, which will be addressed during model training through techniques such as oversampling.

Correlation Matrix

Correlation matrix showing relationships between numerical features in the dataset. Strong correlations with the fraud label (values closer to 1 or -1) indicate potentially useful predictors for fraud detection.

Payment Method Distribution

Distribution of transactions by payment method, showing which payment methods are most common and their associated fraud rates.

Product Category Distribution

Distribution of transactions by product category, showing which product categories are most commonly purchased and their associated fraud rates.

Hourly Transaction Distribution

Distribution of transactions by hour of day, showing peak transaction times and the hourly variation in fraud rates.

Key Insights
What We've Learned
  • The dataset has a significant class imbalance with most transactions being legitimate
  • Transaction patterns vary by payment method, product category, and device used
  • The correlation matrix reveals relationships between numerical features and fraud status
  • Temporal patterns in the time series data may provide additional fraud indicators
